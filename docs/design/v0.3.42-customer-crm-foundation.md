# v0.3.42 customer identity, consent, and CRM foundation

## Purpose

This release introduces one administrative customer record that links product registrations, installations, paid licenses, maintenance, support requests, lifecycle activity, and communication consent. It does not change any desktop feature entitlement or upload receipt content.

## Identity and ownership rules

- Every customer receives a random canonical UUID.
- A paid license and installations reporting that exact managed `license_id` may share a customer record.
- Trial installations are backfilled as separate customers. Matching an unverified email address never merges records automatically.
- Duplicate email hashes are review indicators only. An authorized owner must choose the target, record a business reason, and type `MERGE`; the source remains as a merged record and the merge history is permanent.
- Email verification requires independent ownership confirmation and a recorded audit event. The `customer_email_verifications` table stores only token hashes for the later Customer Portal workflow.

## Data boundaries

The CRM may contain a customer name, canonical email, verification time, license tier and state, maintenance dates, application version, aggregate usage counts, support references, and consent evidence. It must not contain receipt text, raw print bytes, screenshots, diagnostic attachment content, authentication credentials, complete activation keys in customer views or exports, or private-network addresses.

Activation keys receive a SHA-256 fingerprint and a four-character masked ending for exact lookup. The existing operational key ledger remains restricted to the License Manager compatibility boundary; routine customer and API views never query or render the complete key. New or replacement keys are displayed only immediately after confirmed issuance.

## Consent and suppression

- Service messages, marketing, and product analytics are independent purposes.
- Every decision is append-only and records purpose, state, policy version, source, actor, time, and a non-reversible evidence digest.
- A later decision does not edit or delete earlier evidence.
- Unsubscribe, bounce, complaint, closure, and administrative suppression are explicit records. Brevo integration in v0.3.45 must honor the latest consent and every active suppression before queueing mail.

## Administrative authorization

The Admin Portal recognizes `owner`, `support`, and `analyst` roles from protected server configuration. Owner accounts may read, export, record consent, verify ownership, and merge reviewed duplicates. Support accounts may read customer records. Analyst accounts cannot access personal customer records. All accounts still require the existing password and authenticator verification.

Customer CSV export requires the dedicated export capability, CSRF protection, explicit confirmation, and a business reason. The response is non-cacheable and the export event is recorded. Exports exclude activation keys, receipt data, diagnostics, and network addresses.

## Service API

`GET /api/v1/customers.php?id={customer UUID}` is an exact-ID, server-to-server endpoint. It requires a random bearer token whose SHA-256 digest is kept in protected configuration. It does not provide a customer-list endpoint, applies a persistent per-credential/request-source rate limit, returns no full activation keys, and responds with the same not-found result for invalid and unknown identifiers.

## Retention and privacy operations

- **Correction:** update the canonical customer fields while recording who changed them, why, and the prior value digest. A correction must not rewrite license or consent history.
- **Export:** owner-only, reasoned, audited, non-cacheable, and limited to the documented data boundary.
- **Closure:** mark the customer `Closed`, add account-closure email suppression, revoke active portal sessions, and preserve financial, license, consent, and security audit evidence required for legal or fraud-prevention obligations.
- **Deletion:** remove data that has no continuing legal, security, support, or entitlement purpose. Where evidence must remain, replace direct personal fields with irreversible pseudonymous references.
- **Merge reversal:** merges are not silently reversed. A reviewed correction must create new audit evidence and reconstruct links from the permanent merge history.
- **Operational retention:** raw exports are not stored by the application. Support attachments continue to follow the support retention policy. CRM event and audit records are retained only as long as required for ownership, consent, security, accounting, and support evidence.

## Migration and rollback

The schema assurance and backfill are idempotent. Existing license tier, activation key, maintenance expiration, control state, installation token, and usage totals are never changed. If backfill fails, its transaction rolls back. A deployment rollback may hide the Customers page while leaving the additive CRM tables and nullable link columns in place; destructive schema rollback is not required.
