Frequently Asked Questions

Common answers about Windows setup, ESC/POS receipt testing, permanent licenses, annual maintenance, trial limits, and activation.

Product and compatibility

What does POS Printer Emulator do?

It listens for POS receipt print jobs on Windows, renders a live receipt preview, and exposes parsed ESC/POS commands, raw bytes, warnings, and job details for testing and troubleshooting.

Do I need a physical receipt printer?

No. The emulator is specifically designed to let you test receipt output without purchasing or connecting printer hardware.

Which versions of Windows are supported?

The current 64-bit installer supports Windows 10 and Windows 11.

Does it work with Epson receipt printers?

It supports common Epson-compatible ESC/POS workflows and includes guided setup for a TM-T88V-style Windows queue. Model-specific commands can vary, so complete final production validation with the exact hardware.

Does it support every ESC/POS command?

No emulator can assume every vendor extension. The Commands view identifies recognized instructions and flags unsupported data so you know what requires further testing.

Connection and setup

Which network port does it use?

The listener uses TCP port 9100, commonly called RAW printing or the JetDirect-style network-printer port.

Can my POS run on another computer?

Yes. Point the POS to the emulator computer’s local IPv4 address and port 9100. Both devices must be on a trusted network and the Windows firewall must allow the listener.

Can I create a Windows printer queue?

Yes. Use the application’s Printer Setup Wizard or follow the manual Standard TCP/IP port guide. Configure RAW protocol on port 9100.

Why did I receive a blank receipt?

The POS may have sent only initialization or control bytes. Select the job and inspect Raw data and Commands to see exactly what arrived.

Privacy and offline use

Is receipt data stored in the cloud?

No. Receipt contents, raw bytes, and eligible paid-license job history stay on the local Windows device. The service may receive privacy-safe registration, licensing, maintenance-entitlement, and aggregate usage events, but not receipt contents.

Can receipt testing work without internet access?

Core capture, rendering, diagnostics, and local history work locally. Registration, purchase, renewal, update, or account actions may require an internet connection.

Trial, licensing, and maintenance

Release status: v0.3.26 is the current public release and includes Annual Application Maintenance and Support for permanent paid licenses.

What are the Trial Version limits?

The Trial Version allows five emulated print jobs per day, keeps no historical jobs beyond the session, adds a TRIAL watermark, and locks premium features.

What do Lite, Pro, and Enterprise include?

Every paid level permanently includes unlimited print jobs, full local job history, no watermark, exports, printer profiles, Stored Logos, Printer State, and capture/import/replay. Lite allows one total listener, Pro allows two, and Enterprise allows up to 15.

How much do the paid levels cost?

Lite is available at a one-time price of $24.99. Current Pro and Enterprise prices are controlled by the purchase service and shown on the Buy page. Beginning with v0.3.26, optional one-year maintenance renewals are Lite $9.99, Pro $19.99, and Enterprise $59.99.

Is this a software subscription?

No. Lite, Pro, and Enterprise are permanent one-time-purchase licenses. Each new paid purchase includes one year of updates, upgrades, assisted support, and Check for Updates access. Maintenance renewals are optional one-time payments with no automatic recurring billing.

What happens when maintenance expires?

The purchased application, installed version, existing licensed features, history, and listener allowance continue working permanently. Update checks, update downloads, and assisted support pause until renewal. Local troubleshooting information, activation diagnostics, health checks, and privacy-safe log export remain available.

How does maintenance renewal work?

An early renewal adds 12 months to the current expiration date. If coverage has expired, the renewed year starts on the confirmed payment date and restores eligible update and assisted-support access immediately. Paid customers licensed before v0.3.26 received maintenance through July 19, 2027.

How do I activate a paid level?

Purchase a license, open Settings in the installed app, enter your customer or company name, email, and unique activation key, then validate it. Lite, Pro, or Enterprise unlocks immediately without a separate download.

Can I uninstall everything later?

Yes. The Windows uninstaller removes the desktop application, background service, firewall rule, shortcuts, and related application components.
